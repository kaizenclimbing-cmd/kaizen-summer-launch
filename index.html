The summer problem

It's too hot to climb outside. But October's coming fast.

Every year the same thing happens. Summer arrives, conditions get bad, you climb a bit less, train a bit less, and by the time October rolls around — when the rock is finally dry and cool — you're not in the shape you want to be.

August and September are the best months to fix that. The gym is quieter. You've got time. And if you use these 8 weeks properly, you can arrive at October in genuinely good shape — stronger fingers, better fitness, and a clear plan for the season ahead.

Most climbers don't do anything with this window. This programme is for the ones who do.

The structure

Two phases. One goal.

Weeks 1–4
Build the base

Get your body back into training. Rebuild finger strength, general fitness, and movement quality. Sessions designed to be manageable if you've had a lighter summer — not punishing, just progressive.

Weeks 5–8
Peak for October

Intensity increases. You're now converting that base into performance. By the final week you should be climbing well, feeling strong, and ready for whatever you've got planned outdoors.

Who it's for

Boulderers and sport climbers who want to arrive at October ready.

You've had a lighter summer

You've climbed less, trained less. Not through laziness — just life and heat. You want to use the next 8 weeks to actually get back in shape before conditions improve.

You've got a trip or project planned

October in Kalymnos. A sport climbing trip. A boulder problem you've had your eye on. You want to arrive in good shape — not hoping for the best.

You need structure and accountability

You know what to do broadly, but you don't follow it consistently. A plan and a community of people doing the same thing changes that.

You're time-poor but committed

You can't train 5 days a week. You don't need to. The programme is built around real life — minimum effective dose, not maximum volume.
