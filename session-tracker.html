Kaizen Climbing
01
Fingers
~20 min

Because you haven't been training consistently, you don't need to do much to get a good stimulus. Don't try to repeat the numbers from March. You'll either tweak a pulley or crush your ego — or both. Accept that the numbers will be lower. The stimulus is still there.

Start with two hands on a 20mm edge, feet on the floor, and hang for seven seconds. Gradually increase the load over three to five hangs until you're at bodyweight or adding a little weight. Rest about a minute between hangs.

Ramp sets — find your working weight

Once things feel heavy but you're still a few seconds from failure — that's your working weight. 3 sets of 7 seconds at that weight, 2 minutes rest between each. Tick each one off and the rest timer starts automatically.

02
Bouldering
~40 min

The good news is you've already done your main strength work, so now you can enjoy it. Keep the climbing controlled rather than all-out — it'll reduce injury risk and help you move better. Forget the grades. The stimulus matters, not your ego, or trying to impress that cute person at the wall who almost certainly isn't paying attention anyway.

✓
Warm up — work through a couple of problems at each grade until things begin to feel challenging.
✓
Project zone — spend some time on 3–5 problems that take one to three attempts each.
✓
Movement focus — drop a grade and focus purely on movement. Place your feet well. Relax your grip. Climb five easier problems as cleanly as you can.

03
Pull-ups
~10 min

Only if you've still got some energy left — don't force it. Three sets of six, stopping with around three reps still in the tank. You don't need to grind these out. Add weight if bodyweight feels easy, reduce it if it feels hard. Two minutes rest between sets.
