Session Guide

The Summer One-Off Session

For when there's no plan, no training block, and you just want to do something useful. This takes about 75 minutes and leaves you feeling like you actually trained.

Before you start

You haven't been training much. That means you don't need to do much to get a good stimulus.

Don't try to repeat the numbers from March. You'll blow a pulley or crush your ego — or both. Accept that the numbers will be lower. The stimulus is still there.

I climb 9b. This summer I've been doing 6b and 6c circuits. It's the stimulus that counts, not the grade.

The session
01
Fingers
~20 min

Two hands on a 20mm edge. Feet on the floor to start. Gradually increase the load over 3–5 hangs until you're at bodyweight or just above.

  • Hang 5–7 seconds. Rest ~1 minute between hangs.
  • Add weight each hang until it feels heavy but you're still a few seconds from failure — that's your working weight.
  • 3 sets at working weight. 2 minutes rest between sets.
No idea what weight to use? That's the point of the build-up — you're finding where you are right now, not proving anything.
02
Bouldering
~40 min

You've got your strength stimulus done — so you can actually enjoy this. Keep it controlled. This isn't the time to sandbag yourself on hard problems.

  • Work through a couple of problems at each grade until things start to feel challenging.
  • Once you hit that level, spend time on 3–5 problems that might take 1–3 attempts each.
  • Drop a grade. Practise moving well — foot placement, not gripping too tight.
Forget grades entirely. Nobody cares. The person you think is watching definitely isn't.
03
Pull-ups
~10 min

Only if you've got something left. Don't force it.

  • 3 sets of 6.
  • Same principle as the fingers — leave about 3 reps in the tank.
  • Add weight if bodyweight feels easy, reduce if it feels hard.
Total session time ~75 minutes
